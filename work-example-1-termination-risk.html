Work Product Example

Pre-Termination Risk Assessment Tool

The Business Problem
A venture-backed technology company scaling from 50 to 120 employees terminated 8 underperformers across 6 months. Within 90 days, three filed EEOC complaints alleging discrimination. Their "documentation" consisted of Slack messages, vague performance reviews with no specific examples, and subjective assessments like "not a culture fit." Legal counsel estimated $180,000 in defense costs alone—before any settlement. The VP of Engineering realized too late: every termination decision had been made reactively, with no systematic evaluation of legal defensibility.

The company faced mounting pressure to address performance issues quickly as they scaled, but lacked a framework to distinguish between terminations that would withstand legal scrutiny and those that created litigation risk. Each termination consumed 8+ hours of legal review time, delayed critical business decisions, and created anxiety among leadership about "who might sue next."

The Solution
A Pre-Termination Risk Assessment Framework

I developed a structured decision tool that scores legal defensibility across 12 critical risk factors before any termination proceeds. This framework systematically evaluates documentation quality, protected class considerations, timing issues, comparator treatment, and procedural compliance—translating complex legal concepts into actionable decision points for business leaders.

12 Critical Risk Factors

Documentation Quality

High Risk
  • Specific, dated examples of performance failures
  • Written warnings with employee acknowledgment
  • Performance improvement plan with measurable goals
  • Contemporary documentation (not retroactive)

Protected Class Status

High Risk
  • Age 40+ or recent pregnancy/disability disclosure
  • Recent FMLA leave or accommodation request
  • Prior complaints of discrimination or harassment
  • Visible minority status in homogeneous workforce

Timing & Proximity

Medium Risk
  • Within 90 days of protected activity (complaint, leave request)
  • Shortly after performance rating increased or promotion
  • During or immediately after medical/family leave
  • After whistleblowing or policy violation report

Comparator Treatment

High Risk
  • Similarly situated employees treated differently
  • Inconsistent application of performance standards
  • Disparate discipline for same conduct
  • Pattern of terminating specific demographic groups

Procedural Compliance

Medium Risk
  • Progressive discipline policy followed
  • Opportunity to improve provided and documented
  • HR consulted before final decision
  • Severance terms reviewed by counsel

How the Tool Works

  1. Pre-Decision Scoring: Manager completes assessment 48 hours before planned termination discussion
  2. Risk Stratification: Automated scoring categorizes as Low (<20 points), Medium (20-35 points), or High Risk (>35 points)
  3. Escalation Protocol: Medium risk = HR Director review; High risk = Legal counsel + remediation plan before proceeding
  4. Documentation Requirements: Tool generates checklist of missing documentation that must be completed before termination
  5. Decision Tracking: All assessments archived for pattern analysis and legal defensibility
